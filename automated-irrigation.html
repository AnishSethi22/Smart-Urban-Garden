Automated Irrigation Systems: A Comprehensive Guide

Automatic irrigation is the use of a device to operate irrigation structures, allowing the change of water flow from bays to occur without the presence of an irrigator.

Applications of Automation

  • Start and stop irrigation through supply channel outlets
  • Start and stop pumps
  • Cut off water flow from one irrigation area and direct it to another

These changes occur automatically without direct manual effort, but initial setup and maintenance are required.

Disadvantages of Automatic Irrigation

  • Costs for purchasing, installing, and maintaining equipment
  • Reliability issues due to human error during setup
  • Increased maintenance of channels and equipment

Before Installing Automatic Irrigation

Farm Plans Factor in Irrigation

Develop a whole farm plan for your property. Consider automatic irrigation in the planning process to incorporate necessary features from the start.

Installing the Automatic Irrigation

  1. Automate areas chosen for nighttime irrigation to achieve appropriate flow rates.
  2. Automate difficult-to-irrigate areas, such as short steep bays requiring frequent attention.

Things to be Aware Of

Automation is suitable for both laser-graded and non-lasered areas. Channel structures can be purchased and used to automate non-lasered areas with future development in mind.

Choosing the Best Automated Irrigation

Consider the following factors:

  • System cost
  • Backup servicing availability
  • Suitability for your property and irrigation layout

Types of Automatic Irrigation Systems

Pneumatic System

A permanent system activated by a bay sensor at the cut-off point. Water entering the sensor pressurizes air, activating irrigation structures.

Portable Timer System

A temporary system using electronic clocks to activate irrigation structures. Usually, 4-5 units are purchased for use across the property.

Timer or Sensor Hybrid

Combines features of portable timer and sensor systems. Includes a moveable sensor that transmits radio signals to control structures and notify the landowner.

Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA)

Uses a computer and software to schedule and control irrigation via radio link. Can start and stop pumps and motors.

Automating an Irrigation Layout

Automation of Channel Sections

Automates channel structures to change water levels. Requires significant fall in the channel system.

Automation of Individual Bay Outlets

Controls bay outlets to change water flow. Most frequently used where insufficient fall exists for channel section automation.

The same automatic devices can be set up to operate either channel sections or bay outlets automation.
